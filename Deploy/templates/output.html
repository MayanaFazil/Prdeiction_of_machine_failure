Overstrain Failure occurs when a mechanical component or material is subjected to stress levels beyond its designed limits. This excessive stress can result from factors such as overloading, sudden shocks, or sustained forces. This type of failure typically leads to deformation or structural damage in the affected component, making it unable to perform its intended function effectively. It's a common issue in engineering and structural design, and engineers often use safety margins to prevent overstrain failures. Preventing Overstrain Failure is crucial in industries where safety is paramount, such as aerospace and construction, as it can have catastrophic consequences if not properly addressed. Effective monitoring and inspection techniques are essential for identifying signs of overstrain in components, allowing for timely maintenance or replacement to prevent failure. Engineers employ various methods, including finite element analysis and stress testing, to assess and mitigate the risk of overstrain failure in critical structures and components.

Tool wear failure is a common issue in machining and manufacturing processes. It occurs when cutting tools used in machinery gradually deteriorate over time due to friction and abrasive forces.
